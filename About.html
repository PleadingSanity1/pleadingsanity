We Rise From Madness

Pleading Sanity was born from the chaos — the sleepless nights, the bruises you can’t see, the moments when hope was just a rumour. We are not polished. We are not perfect. We are proof that broken things still shine.

“They say it’s the genius disease. Maybe they’re right. But this movement wasn’t built for the spotlight — it was built for survival, for leaving something real behind for my kids, and for everyone who never got to tell their story.”
— Shane, Founder

We believe pain is a superpower if you survive it. Every hoodie, every podcast, every game, every story in the Sanity Hub is a reminder: you are not alone in this fight.

What Makes Us Different

  • Unfiltered Truth: We don’t hide the scars. Here, your story is currency, your struggle is welcome, and your comeback is celebrated.
  • Built By Survivors: This isn’t corporate — it’s crafted by those who lived it. Mental health isn’t a hashtag; it’s the frontline of our lives.
  • Cosmic Community: Our goal is a galaxy of healing: streetwear with a story, gyms that welcome the broken, AI tools that actually listen, and a feed that feeds your soul.

This Movement Is For You

When you join Pleading Sanity, you’re not buying into hype. You’re helping build safe spaces for anyone who feels “too much” — and creating tools, content, and culture that give back.

We’re building what we never had: a place where survival is respected, pain is transformed, and support is more than words on a page.

This is Pleading Sanity — the movement where you become the message.
Where your comeback becomes someone else’s survival guide.
Where our madness builds a legacy, and our scars become our badge of honor.
Step inside the Sanity Hub, or wear the change — and help us turn the world’s pain into a movement for hope.

Why Now?

Because the world is drowning in fake “motivation.” Because there’s no app for real hope. Because no brand is telling the raw truth — but we will. With your help, we’ll build gyms, launch podcasts, and drop gear that funds real healing. We’re not just here to talk — we’re here to change the story, one survivor at a time.
